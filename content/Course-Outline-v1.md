## Program Overview

A self-paced program that prepares international entrepreneurs to successfully launch and operate a business in the United States, with a focus on the Central Florida ecosystem.

**This is not a traditional course.** It's the entry point to a structured pathway:
- **Phase 1** (this program): Education + readiness — learn what you need to know
- **Phase 2** (future): Ecosystem introduction — warm intros, guided navigation, advisory
- **Phase 3** (future): Full incubator membership — coaching, mentorship, execution support

**Target audience:** International founders vetted by UCF BIP — established businesses expanding to the U.S., primarily from Latin America, Europe, and Asia.

**Estimated time commitment:** 2-3 hours total, self-paced

---

## Module 1 — Establishing Your U.S. Business

*The legal and operational foundation for setting up shop in the United States.*

Everything an international founder needs to know to go from "I want to do business here" to having a real, operational U.S. entity.

**Topics covered:**
- Choosing the right business entity (LLC, C-Corp, S-Corp) — and why it matters
- Registering your business in Florida (Sunbiz)
- Obtaining your EIN (Employer Identification Number)
- Opening a U.S. business bank account as a foreign national
- Initial compliance requirements
- Insurance expectations — what U.S. partners and clients will expect
- Contract risk — how U.S. contract law differs from what you know
- Regulatory exposure — industry-specific requirements you may not anticipate
- Operational liability — protecting yourself personally

**Outcome:** A clear legal U.S. presence roadmap — the step-by-step sequence to get legally established.

---

## Module 2 — How U.S. Business Actually Works

*The unwritten rules that no one tells you — until you get it wrong.*

The cultural and operational norms that catch international founders off guard. This isn't theory — it's what you need to know to be taken seriously in U.S. business interactions.

**Topics covered:**
- Speed of decision-making — why Americans move fast and expect you to
- Direct communication norms — what "honest feedback" looks like here
- Sales expectations — how U.S. buyers evaluate vendors and partners
- Trust and credibility signals — what builds (and destroys) your reputation
- Meeting etiquette and relationship-building — it's different here
- Founder vs. manager expectations — what the market expects of you
- Negotiation style differences — directness, timelines, follow-through
- Common cultural missteps — real examples from international founders

**Outcome:** A cultural readiness checklist — concrete adjustments to make before your first U.S. business meeting.

---

## Module 3 — Protecting Your Business

*Intellectual property, contracts, and knowing when to bring in a professional.*

The U.S. has strong IP protections — but only if you use them. This module covers what international founders commonly miss and what can cost you everything if you ignore it.

**Topics covered:**
- Overview of U.S. intellectual property: copyright, trademark, patent
- Common IP mistakes international founders make
- When and how to bring in an attorney — and what that costs
- Protecting your business idea during early conversations

**Outcome:** An IP risk awareness plan — know what to protect, how, and when to get help.

---

## Module 4 — Taxes & Financial Fundamentals

*What you need to know about money in the U.S. — before you learn it the hard way.*

U.S. tax and accounting practices are different from almost everywhere else. This module gives you the baseline financial literacy to avoid expensive surprises.

**Topics covered:**
- Federal vs. state taxes — yes, you deal with both
- Sales tax realities — it varies by state, county, and product
- Accounting expectations — why U.S. bookkeeping differs from global norms
- Working with a U.S. accountant — what to expect and what it costs
- Financial compliance basics for foreign-owned entities

**Outcome:** Basic understanding of U.S.-specific financial obligations so you can have an informed conversation with an accountant.

---

## Module 5 — Going to Market in the U.S.

*Why your product probably won't transfer directly — and what to do about it.*

What works in your home market may not work here. This module covers the realities of U.S. customer acquisition and market entry strategy.

**Topics covered:**
- Why products and services rarely transfer directly to the U.S. market
- U.S. customer acquisition expectations — sales cycles, pricing, trust
- Adapting your value proposition for American buyers
- Location and market strategy — why Central Florida, industry clusters

**Outcome:** A realistic assessment of what needs to change in your product/service and go-to-market approach for the U.S.

---

## Module 6 — Hiring & Immigration Basics

*Your first U.S. hire, visa pathways, and what you can't figure out from Google.*

If you're planning to operate in the U.S., you'll eventually need people. This module covers the basics of hiring, the contractor-vs-employee distinction that trips up every foreign founder, and an honest overview of visa pathways.

**Topics covered:**
- Hiring your first U.S. employee — what's involved
- Contractor vs. employee — the legal distinction that matters enormously
- Visa pathway overview — O-1, E-2, L-1, and what's realistic
- Payroll expectations and employer obligations

**Outcome:** Enough knowledge to plan your first hire and understand your immigration options. Sets the stage for deeper Phase 2 advisory.

---

## Module 7 — The Central Florida Ecosystem

*You don't have to do this alone — here's who can help.*

An awareness-level introduction to the organizations, programs, and resources available to international entrepreneurs in Central Florida. This is not a how-to guide — it's a menu of what exists so you know what to ask about.

**Topics covered:**
- Key organizations: SBDC, SBA, chambers of commerce
- Economic development agencies (Orlando Economic Partnership, SelectFlorida)
- University partnerships — how schools like UCF support businesses
- Industry-specific resources and trade organizations (e.g., CFITO for LATAM trade)
- UCF Business Incubation Program — where this fits in the ecosystem
- Hispanic Chamber, Prospera, and other community-specific resources (where applicable)

**Outcome:** A list of support organizations and their specialties — know who to contact and what they offer.
