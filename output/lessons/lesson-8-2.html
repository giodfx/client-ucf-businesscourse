Did You Know?

The UCF Business Incubation Program operates multiple sites across the greater Orlando region, providing over 100,000 square feet of incubation space. Since its founding, companies in the program have created thousands of jobs in Central Florida. UCF itself is one of the top patent-producing universities in the country, and the research partnerships between campus labs and incubator companies give founders access to talent and technology that would cost a fortune to build independently.

Resource 2: Florida Setup Roadmap

This is your step-by-step sequence — what comes first, second, third. The Florida Setup Roadmap is sorted by order instead of topic, answering exactly that question. It walks through four phases: (1) pre-arrival preparation (remote), (2) entity formation and registration, (3) financial infrastructure, and (4) operational readiness. Each phase includes estimated timeframes and flags which steps require physical presence versus remote completion.

Many founders complete phases 1 and 2 remotely before arriving, so they can open a bank account in one visit with all documents ready.

It covers business advising (SBDC, SCORE, UCF BIP), incubators and accelerators, government programs (SBA, Enterprise Florida), professional services (attorneys, CPAs, banking contacts), networking and community organizations, and details on what comes after this course through UCF BIP.

Resource 4: Expansion Decision Worksheet

This is your go/no-go decision — the honest assessment before you commit. The Expansion Decision Worksheet helps you decide whether to enter the U.S. market now. It scores you across five areas: market fit, financial readiness, legal readiness, operations, and timing.

This is not pass/fail. A founder from Colombia scored 2 out of 5 on financial readiness, spent four months strengthening home-market revenue, re-scored at 4, and entered the U.S. with a much stronger foundation. The worksheet tells you what gap to close first.

Complete this before your 1:1 meeting with UCF BIP — it helps the team give you targeted advice based on your specific readiness profile.

Your Digital Credential

Your completion credential — proof you finished the program. When you complete this program, you earn a digital credential : a verified badge for your LinkedIn profile, website, or email signature. It validates completion of the UCF BIP U.S. Market Readiness Program, backed by one of the largest and most active university incubation ecosystems in the country.

Now, here is where it gets tricky. The credential is issued through POK Tech, the same provider used by Arizona State University and other major universities. It is a program completion credential, not a professional certification — but it signals you took a structured, university-backed program seriously.

How to Claim Your Credential

After completing the program, you receive an email from POK Tech. The process takes five minutes:

  1. Open the email and select the claim link
  2. Set up your POK Tech account (or log in)
  3. Accept the credential
  4. Share to LinkedIn using the built-in sharing tool

The credential does not expire.

Question 1 of 3

A founder has validated her market opportunity and wants to know the correct ORDER of steps to become operational in Florida — from entity formation through financial setup to hiring. Which downloadable resource is specifically designed for this purpose?

The Florida Setup Roadmap is organized by sequence rather than by topic. It walks founders through four phases : pre-arrival preparation, entity formation, financial infrastructure, and operational readiness — in the correct order. The Checklist organizes by module topic, while the Roadmap answers 'what do I do first, second, third?'

A founder completed the Expansion Decision Worksheet and scored high on market fit but low on financial readiness. Based on the lesson guidance, what is the most strategic response?

The Expansion Decision Worksheet is not a pass/fail test. A founder from Colombia used it, found her financial score was 2 out of 5, spent four months strengthening home-market revenue, and re-scored at 4 before entering the U.S. with a stronger foundation. The worksheet tells you what gap to close first, not whether to give up.

A founder from Brazil has completed the UCF BIP Phase 1 program and wants to use her digital credential to strengthen her professional profile when meeting potential U.S. partners. How should she apply the POK Tech credential?

The POK Tech digital credential is designed to be shared digitally — on LinkedIn, websites, and email signatures. It validates completion of the UCF BIP U.S. Market Readiness Program and does not expire. It is a program completion credential, not a professional certification, so it does not qualify for SBA lending or serve as a formal qualification. There is no reason to wait for Phase 2 — the Phase 1 credential stands on its own as a signal of structured preparation.
