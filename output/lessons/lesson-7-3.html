Measuring the Flow, Central Florida by the Numbers

In the last lesson you explored the culture and connections of Central Florida's business ecosystem — the collaborative mindset, the bilingual community, the people who answer emails. Now it is time to look at the numbers that back it up. A natural spring's strength is measured by how many gallons per second it pushes to the surface. An ecosystem's strength is measured by what it produces.

Numbers tell a story most pitch decks miss. A $500,000 seed fund buys you 10 months in Silicon Valley, but 17 months in Central Florida. That difference can mean the gap between finding product-market fit and running out of cash. This lesson is the spreadsheet: the numbers to cite when building a case for your board, co-founder, or family. All data as of early 2026 unless noted.

Population and Growth

Let's break this down. Orlando-Kissimmee-Sanford metro: ~2.8 million residents. Top five fastest-growing U.S. metro over the past five years : driving consumer demand, worker supply, and commercial real estate growth.

Regional GDP

Metro Orlando GDP exceeds $150 billion annually, spread across tourism ($75B), tech, aerospace, health care, education, and construction.

Business Formation

Florida handles 300,000+ new business filings each year via SunBiz — top three nationally. Florida LLC filing: $125. For reference: California charges $70 plus an $800 annual franchise tax, New York charges $200 plus biennial requirements. Florida's zero state income tax covers all pass-through business income.

Did You Know?

Florida's natural springs collectively discharge more than 8 billion gallons of water per day, enough to fill thousands of Olympic swimming pools. That constant, reliable flow sustains entire river systems and the wildlife that depends on them. Central Florida's economy works with a similar kind of momentum: 300,000+ new business filings per year, $150 billion in annual GDP, and a population growing faster than most U.S. metros. The numbers just keep flowing.

What Would You Do?

Question 1 of 3

A founder has $500,000 in seed funding and burns $50,000/month in Silicon Valley. Based on the cost data in this lesson, approximately how many EXTRA months of runway would she gain by relocating to Central Florida?

At $50,000/month in Silicon Valley versus approximately $30,000/month in Central Florida (based on the salary, office, and housing differentials in the cost comparison), the founder saves roughly $20,000/month. On $500,000 in funding, that extends runway from 10 months (Silicon Valley) to approximately 16-17 months (Central Florida) — roughly 7 extra months. That is the difference between finding product-market fit and running out of cash.

Which Central Florida industry cluster generates approximately $6 billion in annual contracts and is anchored by a U.S. Navy command headquarters?

The Simulation and Training cluster generates ~$6B in annual contracts and is anchored by NAWCTSD (Naval Air Warfare Center Training Systems Division), making Orlando the global hub for modeling, simulation, and training. The cluster includes 200+ companies and is expanding into commercial applications like VR and surgical simulation.

A founder from Argentina has a three-person team and $200,000 in capital. She is comparing Central Florida and New York City as her U.S. base. Based on the cost data in this lesson, which analysis best captures the financial difference?

The data shows a dramatic cost gap: COL index 98 vs. 187, developer salaries of $85K-$100K vs. $120K-$145K, office space at $25-$35/sq ft vs. $60-$85/sq ft, and apartments at $1,400-$1,800 vs. $2,800-$4,000. While vehicle costs are a real trade-off, they do not erase the savings. And New York does charge state income tax (4%-10.9%), unlike Florida.

Key Takeaway

Central Florida's cost of living index (98) is nearly half of New York's (187) and well below Silicon Valley's (172). Developer salaries run $85K-$100K versus $130K-$160K in San Jose. Florida handles 300,000+ new business filings each year with a $125 LLC fee and zero state income tax. Six diversified industry clusters — from simulation and training ($6B) to aerospace (28 launches in 2025) to hospitality tech ($75B+ tourism GDP) — create demand across sectors.
