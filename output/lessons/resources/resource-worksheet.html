Structured go/no-go framework • Score yourself honestly across 5 dimensions

This is your honest self-assessment before committing to U.S. market entry. Score each dimension from 1 (Not Ready) to 5 (Fully Ready). There are no wrong answers — a low score tells you what to strengthen before entering, not that you should give up.

A founder from Colombia scored 2/5 on financial readiness, spent four months strengthening home-market revenue, re-scored at 4, and entered the U.S. with a much stronger foundation.

4Pilot customers or LOIs from U.S. buyers. Clear value proposition adapted for market.
5Active paying U.S. customers. Product-market fit validated with real revenue.

3 Legal Readiness

4 Operations
1No U.S. contacts, infrastructure, or plan for physical/virtual presence.
2Can operate remotely from home country. No U.S.-based team or workspace.
3Coworking or virtual office secured. Can handle U.S. time zones. Some local contacts.
4U.S.-based team member or partner. Workspace secured. Supply chain identified.
5Full U.S. operations running. Team hired. Systems, vendors, and workspace in place.
5 Timing
1Home business is unstable. Personal circumstances make relocation impossible now.
2Home market needs attention. U.S. market is interesting but no urgency or window.
3Home business is stable. U.S. market opportunity exists. No blocking personal factors.
4Market window is open. Competitive timing is good. Personal situation supports the move.
5Clear market window, partner/customer pulling you in, home ops self-sustaining.
