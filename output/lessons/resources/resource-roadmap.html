Florida Setup Roadmap

UCF Business Incubation Program — Market Readiness Course

Step-by-step sequence from entity formation to fully operational

Remote In-Person Either
Phase 1 Pre-Arrival Preparation 2–4 weeks before arrival
1
Research entity type — Compare LLC vs. C-Corp vs. S-Corp based on your visa status, investor plans, and tax goals. Remote
Key decision: C-Corp if seeking VC; LLC for simplicity; S-Corp generally unavailable to non-residents.
2
Secure a registered agent — Hire a Florida-based registered agent service to receive legal mail. Remote
Cost: $100–$300/year. Required before filing with SunBiz.
3
Apply for EIN online — File Form SS-4 on IRS.gov. International applicants can call IRS at (267) 941-1099. Remote
Free. Takes 15 minutes online (or ~30 min by phone for non-U.S. SSN holders).
4
Draft operating agreement or bylaws — Define ownership structure, profit distribution, and management rights. Remote
Tip: Use a Florida business attorney. LegalZoom/Incfile offer templates for simpler setups.
5
Gather banking documents — Prepare passport, EIN confirmation, formation docs, and proof of address. Remote
Some banks (Mercury, Relay) accept fully remote applications with these documents.
Phase 2 Entity Formation & Registration Week 1–2 after decision
6
File Articles of Organization/Incorporation — Submit on SunBiz.org. LLC = Articles of Organization; Corp = Articles of Incorporation. Either
Filing fee: ~$125 (LLC) or ~$70 (Corp) + registered agent designation.
7
Obtain local business tax receipt — Register with your county (Orange, Seminole, Osceola, etc.) and city if required. Either
Cost varies by jurisdiction. Orange County uses an online portal.
8
Register for Florida sales tax — Apply through the FL Department of Revenue if selling taxable goods or services. Remote
Free registration. Permits typically arrive within 5 business days.
9
Apply for required industry permits — Check if your industry requires state or federal licensing (food, healthcare, construction, etc.). Either
Florida DBPR handles most professional licenses. Use the SBDC for guidance.
Phase 3 Financial Infrastructure Week 2–4
10
Open a business bank account — Apply with Mercury or Relay (remote-friendly), or visit a local bank branch with all documents. Either
Bring: EIN, Articles, operating agreement, passport, proof of address. Some banks require in-person visit.
11
Set up accounting software — Configure QuickBooks Online (or Xero/FreshBooks), connect bank account, set chart of accounts. Remote
Record every transaction from day one. Your CPA will need clean books at tax time.
12
Set up payment processing — Register with Stripe, Square, or PayPal Business for accepting customer payments. Remote
Stripe Atlas includes company formation + banking + payment processing in one bundle.
13
Hire a CPA — Find an accountant experienced with international-owned U.S. businesses and estimated quarterly taxes. Either
Ask UCF BIP or SBDC for referrals. Budget $1,500–$5,000/year for small business accounting.
14
Set up quarterly estimated tax payments — Calculate and schedule federal estimated tax payments (due Apr 15, Jun 15, Sep 15, Jan 15). Remote
Florida has no state income tax. Federal payments via EFTPS.gov.
Phase 4 Operational Readiness Month 2–3
15
Obtain General Liability insurance — Shop quotes from at least 3 carriers. Most landlords and clients require proof of GL. Either
Typical cost: $500–$1,500/year for a startup. Use an independent insurance broker.
16
Secure workspace — Evaluate coworking spaces, UCF BIP incubator space, or traditional office leases. In-Person
UCF BIP offers below-market incubator space with mentorship. StarterStudio, Canvs, and WeWork are popular alternatives.
17
Set up payroll (if hiring) — Register with a payroll provider (Gusto, ADP, OnPay) and set up new-hire reporting. Remote
Florida requires new-hire reporting within 20 days. Register for reemployment tax on FL DOR.
18
Build your advisory network — Connect with SBDC, SCORE mentor, UCF BIP advisor, and at least one peer founder. Either
All three programs are free. Schedule intake meetings within your first 2 weeks.
19
Launch your U.S. market presence — Go live with website, social profiles, and initial outreach to target customers. Remote
Tip: Start selling before everything is "perfect." Revenue validates your market fit faster than any plan.
