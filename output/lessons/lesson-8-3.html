What Would You Do?

Question 1 of 3

A founder has completed Phase 1 (this program), landed her first two U.S. customers, and generated early revenue. Which UCF BIP phase is designed for her current business stage?

Phase 3 of the UCF BIP market entry/soft landing program is specifically designed for founders who already have initial traction : early customers, revenue, or signed partnerships. It focuses on growth strategy, investor connections, expanded networking, and operational support for scaling. Phase 2 is for founders still establishing initial operations.

According to the lesson's guidance on effective goal-setting, which of the following is the STRONGEST action item for a founder's 30-day plan?

Effective action items must be specific, time-bound, and completable. 'Schedule a 30-minute consultation with a CPA who works with foreign-owned businesses by March 28' includes a specific action (schedule consultation), a specific qualifier (CPA with international experience), and a deadline. Vague goals like 'learn more' or 'research the market' lack the specificity needed for follow-through.

A founder from Guatemala has completed this Phase 1 program and wants to take action this week. She has not yet formed a U.S. entity, has no SBDC appointment, and has not completed the Expansion Decision Worksheet. Based on the lesson's guidance on prioritizing next steps, which specific set of actions should she take in the next seven days?

The lesson emphasizes three specific first-week actions: book the UCF BIP 1:1 meeting (free, 30 minutes, the single most important next step), connect with a support resource like SBDC, and complete the Expansion Decision Worksheet so the BIP team can give targeted advice. Filing an LLC before consulting a professional risks choosing the wrong entity type. Waiting for full confidence delays progress unnecessarily. Networking matters, but structured support should come first.
