Registering with SunBiz — Step by Step

From Entity to Registration — Making It Official

Now that you have chosen your business structure — whether an LLC or a C-Corp — it is time to make it official. Florida is where most international founders in this program will register, and the process is straightforward once you know the steps. The Division of Corporations, known as SunBiz, handles all business filings online. You do not need to be physically present in Florida to complete this process.

ℹ

Did You Know?

Florida LLC formation through SunBiz can be completed entirely online — you do not need to be physically in the United States to file. Some founders have registered their entity from another country in the morning and had it active before the end of the week. Very few U.S. states offer this mix of speed, ease, and remote access.

SunBiz — Florida's Division of Corporations

SunBiz (sunbiz.org) is Florida's official portal for registering business entities, filing annual reports, and updating records. Think of sunbiz.org as City Hall's online counter — you walk in, file your papers, and walk out with your business on the city registry.

Here's how this plays out. Before you begin, you typically need three things:

  1. Your entity type decision. LLC or Corporation? If you have not decided yet, review the previous lesson.
  2. A business name. It must be distinguishable from other Florida businesses and include a designator ("LLC," "Inc.," or "Corp."). You can check availability on SunBiz before filing.
  3. A registered agent. A person or service with a physical Florida street address (not a P.O. box) to receive legal documents on your behalf.

Here is the general process:

  1. Go to sunbiz.org and select "Start a Business."
  2. Choose your entity type.
  3. Enter your business name (availability is typically checked in real time).
  4. Provide member or manager information.
  5. Designate your registered agent with their Florida street address.
  6. Enter your principal and mailing addresses.
  7. Pay the filing fee — typically around $125 for an LLC or $70 for a Corporation.
  8. Submit. Your entity is often active within a few business days.

With your information ready, the process can usually be completed in about 20 to 30 minutes.

EIN — Your Business's Tax ID

After SunBiz registration, your next step is an Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the IRS. Your EIN is like the address number on your storefront — without it, no one can find you in the system. You will need it for bank accounts, tax filings, hiring, and business licenses. The EIN is free.

The short version: With an SSN or ITIN: You can typically submit your request online at irs.gov. The process takes about 15 minutes and the EIN is often issued right away.

Without an SSN or ITIN: You will need to complete Form SS-4 by fax (which may take several business days) or by mail (which may take several weeks). The fastest alternative: call the IRS international line (267-941-1099), available Monday through Friday, 6 AM to 11 PM Eastern. Same-day processing has been reported, though wait times can vary a lot — some founders report being on hold for an hour or more, while others get through in minutes. Calling early in the morning tends to have shorter wait times. Verify this number is still active before relying on it, as IRS contact options can change.

A useful tip: you can request an ITIN when filing your first tax return, which opens up the online EIN process for future entities.

The EIN belongs to the business, not to you personally. Each company needs its own EIN. Keep your confirmation letter (CP 575) — banks and clients on W-9 forms will ask for it.

Registered Agent and Address — The Physical Presence Requirement

Florida requires every business entity to maintain a registered agent with a physical street address — not a P.O. box, not a virtual mailbox. Think of the registered agent as the doorman at your building address — someone who is there to receive official notices when you are not.

Addresses on SunBiz

Let's put this in perspective. SunBiz asks for three addresses:

  • Principal address: A coworking space, office, or incubator
  • Mailing address: A virtual mailbox service (often $10-$30/month) or same as your principal address
  • Registered agent address: A professional agent service, or your principal address if someone is physically present during business hours

UCF BIP offers flexible office space that founders can use as their business address. Ask your UCF BIP contact about availability.

Registered Agent Services

If you are not in Florida full-time, a registered agent service is often the simplest solution. These typically run $100-$300 per year. Several well-known providers offer this service online.

Don't overlook this step. If your registered agent lapses, Florida can dissolve your entity, which means your business legally stops existing until you fix it.

Common Filing Errors That Delay Your Registration

Here's where founders lose time. These are the mistakes that most commonly cause SunBiz filings to be rejected or delayed:

  • Name already taken. Always check availability on SunBiz before you submit. If your preferred name is taken, consider adding an industry descriptor or geographic identifier. Also check whether the matching website domain name is available — once your entity name is filed with SunBiz, you cannot change it without forming a new entity.
  • Wrong entity suffix. LLCs must include "LLC" or "L.L.C." in the name. Corporations must include "Inc.," "Corp.," or "Co." Filing without the correct suffix may be rejected.
  • Missing registered agent designation. The registered agent must consent to serve in that role. If the information does not match what the agent has on file with the state, the filing may stall.
  • Incorrect address format. SunBiz requires a street address for the principal and registered agent addresses. A P.O. box is not accepted for these fields. International addresses are generally acceptable for the mailing address.

Watch Your Money — What Registration Actually Costs

Notice how registration-related costs can add up faster than expected. Here are typical ranges:

  • SunBiz filing fee: Typically around $125 for an LLC or $70 for a Corporation.
  • Registered agent service: Typically $100–$300 per year if you are not in Florida full-time.
  • Virtual mailbox (mailing address): Often $10–$30 per month.
  • Operating agreement (attorney-drafted): Often $300–$800 for an LLC. DIY templates are available, but may miss details specific to foreign-owned entities.
  • EIN application: Free from the IRS — no reason to pay a third party for this.

The most common overspend at this stage is paying a service hundreds to thousands of dollars for something you can do yourself on SunBiz in about 30 minutes. The filing itself is simple. Where expert help truly adds value is drafting an operating agreement, setting up corporate bylaws, or advising on entity type. Save your budget for those talks.

What Would You Do?

Question 1 of 3

You are ready to register your LLC on SunBiz, but you are currently living outside the United States. What is the most important thing to arrange before you begin the filing?

Florida requires every business to have a registered agent with a physical street address. If you are not in Florida, a registered agent service handles this for you. A driver's license is not required for SunBiz filing. A personal bank account is a separate step that comes after entity formation. An office lease is not a prerequisite for registration.

You just registered your Florida LLC on SunBiz. Before you finalize, a mentor tells you to also check the website domain name. Why does this matter?

Your SunBiz entity name cannot be changed after filing without forming a new entity. If you discover the matching domain is unavailable after filing, you will either need a mismatched web presence or a new entity. The domain does not need to match exactly for the LLC to be valid, SunBiz does not reserve domains, and waiting until marketing starts risks discovering a conflict too late. Checking domain availability before filing takes two minutes and can save significant hassle.

You need an EIN for your new Florida LLC but you do not have an SSN or ITIN. What is typically the fastest way to get one?

Without an SSN or ITIN, the online application is not available. Calling the IRS international line at 267-941-1099 has been reported as the fastest alternative, though wait times can vary. The online method requires an SSN or ITIN you do not have. Mailing the form can take several weeks. Registered agents do not typically file EIN applications on your behalf. Verify the phone number is current before calling.

Key Takeaway

The takeaway is this: your trip to City Hall is one of the simplest stops in the city. Florida registration through SunBiz is one of the most straightforward steps in your U.S. setup process. The real preparation happens before you file: choosing the right entity type, securing a registered agent, and understanding the address requirements.

Florida's mix of no state personal income tax, simple online filing, global ties, and lower costs is a big part of why founders keep choosing it as their entry point. Apply this knowledge before you click "submit": have your EIN plan, registered agent, and address strategy ready. Compare Florida's advantages to other states, then evaluate whether the timing is right for your filing.
