Your Readiness Self-Assessment

Before you leave headquarters and head into the U.S. market, you need a readiness review. This is yours. For each topic, honestly rate yourself: clear on what to do, or still have major questions? No passing score. The point is clarity.

Module 1: Legal Setup

Is your foundation solid? Entity, EIN, registered agent — these are the structural systems that keep everything else from collapsing.

  • Do you know which entity type fits your business goals and visa situation?
  • Have you checked SunBiz for name availability?
  • Do you understand the registered agent requirement?
  • Do you know what documents you need for a U.S. business bank account?

If unclear, consult a business attorney or CPA before filing.

Module 2: Finances and Taxes

Are your accounts and funding in order? Banking, taxes, accounting — these power everything that follows once you step out of headquarters.

  • Can you explain federal income tax vs. state sales tax?
  • Do you understand how U.S. sales tax differs from VAT?
  • Have you identified an accountant who understands international founders?

If unclear, an international-focused accountant saves you from penalties that cost far more than their fee.

Module 3: Protecting Your Business

Are your legal protections in place? IP, insurance, and contracts are your safety net — invisible until you need them, catastrophic without them.

  • Does your home-country IP protection cover you in the U.S.?
  • Can you list the insurance types your business needs?
  • Have you identified which contracts you need before starting?

If uncertain, an IP attorney conversation is a high-priority next step.

Module 4: Building Your Team

Is your team assembled? Classification, payroll, compliance — your support team is critical, and getting the roster wrong stalls you before you start.

  • Do you understand contractor vs. employee in the U.S.?
  • Are you aware of true hiring cost (salary plus 15-30% overhead)?
  • Do you know your visa authorization status?

Misclassifying a worker can result in back taxes, penalties, and legal liability.

Readiness Check — Markets, Culture, and Ecosystem

Now, let's continue with the remaining modules.

Module 5: Understanding U.S. Customers

Is your market strategy configured for the U.S.? Product adaptation, pricing, acquisition strategy — these determine whether your business actually reaches your target market.

  • Have you researched your U.S. competitive set?
  • Do you know how U.S. pricing expectations differ from your home market?
  • Do you understand vendor onboarding requirements (W-9, insurance certificates, procurement)?
  • Can you describe your first U.S. customer acquisition strategy?

If you haven't done U.S.-specific competitive research, block time for that before selling.

Module 6: U.S. Business Culture

Have you studied the local conditions? Communication, meetings, trust-building — this is the atmosphere you will be operating in, and misjudging it stalls even the best-prepared founders.

  • Can you explain American friendliness vs. business commitment?
  • Do you know follow-up expectations after meetings?
  • Are you aware of how directness and decision-making differ in U.S. business?

Cultural missteps cost real money. If these feel abstract, find a mentor who has operated in both cultures.

Module 7: Central Florida Ecosystem

Do you know your advisory network? SBDC, chambers, UCF BIP — no founder succeeds without a support team on the ground.

  • Can you name three organizations offering free support to international founders?
  • Do you know Central Florida's advantages vs. Miami, NYC, or Silicon Valley?
  • Are you aware of what phases 2 and 3 of the UCF BIP program offer?

If you cannot name specific resources you plan to contact, revisit Module 7.

Where Do You Need More Help?

No preparation is perfect. The smartest founders identify what is missing and get help before they leave headquarters. Feeling swamped is normal. You are not supposed to be an expert in all seven areas. That is exactly why UCF's campus has an entire ecosystem of advisors, mentors, and professional connections waiting in the buildings around you. You need to know enough to ask the right questions and avoid expensive mistakes.

Where to Get Help

  • Legal (entity, contracts, compliance) . Business attorney with international experience. SBDC connects you with pro bono legal clinics.
  • Taxes and accounting . CPA with international experience. Ask: "Do you work with foreign-owned businesses?"
  • Immigration and visas . Immigration attorney. Not optional.
  • IP protection . IP attorney. USPTO has resources, but filing strategy needs professional guidance.
  • Banking . Start with the bank directly. Bring documents; expect the first visit to be a consultation.
  • Customer acquisition . SBDC and SCORE offer free advising. UCF BIP connects you with mentors.
  • General guidance . The UCF BIP team is available for a conversation once you complete Module 8.

Your readiness scores are your map. Prioritize professionals based on your lowest scores.
