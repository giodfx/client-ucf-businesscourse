## 1. Executive Summary

This revised proposal reflects our discovery conversations and Brian's feedback on the scope, goals, and strategic direction of this engagement.

The original proposal included two deliverables: a lead generation platform ($2,000) and a course ($7,500). Since the lead generation system is being handled through a separate internal solution, this proposal focuses exclusively on the course development effort at the agreed **$7,500 investment**.

However, based on our discussions, what UCF BIP needs is not a standard course. It is a **U.S. Market Readiness Program** — an educational experience that also functions as a qualification tool, a data collection system, and the entry point to a longer-term engagement with international entrepreneurs.

This proposal reflects that expanded vision while staying within the agreed budget, made possible by leveraging our proprietary development platform to deliver capabilities that would otherwise require significant custom development or expensive third-party tools.

## 3. What We Are Building

### The U.S. Market Readiness Program — Phase 1

This is the first phase of what Brian has described as a 3-phase platform journey:

- **Phase 1** — Online Course (what this proposal covers): Self-paced education + business profiling + qualification routing
- **Phase 2** — Ecosystem Introduction (future, manual): Warm introductions, guided navigation, one-on-one sessions
- **Phase 3** — Incubator Membership (existing): Full UCF BIP program participation

We are building Phase 1. But it is designed as an on-ramp — not a dead end. Every learner who completes Phase 1 exits with a clear next step, and UCF BIP exits with usable data about who they are and what they need.

### The Learner Journey

```
INTAKE
   Business profile: 5-8 questions
   (stage, industry, country, goals, language)
          │
          ▼
CORE COURSE
   Module 1: Getting Started in the U.S.
   Module 2: U.S. Business Culture
   Module 3: Protecting Your Business (IP)
   Module 4: Finding Support & Resources
   Module 5: Taxes & Financial Fundamentals
          │
          ▼
REFLECTION & DATA CAPTURE
   What are your top priorities?
   What questions remain?
   What are your planned next steps?
          │
          ▼
EXIT ROUTING (based on profile + reflection)
   Path A: Exit — Resources provided, not a fit for BIP right now
   Path B: Learn More — Route to UCF BIP program overview
   Path C: Connect — Schedule 15/30/60 min with Brian
```

### Content Modules

The following modules reflect the agreed outline plus the highest-priority addition from Brian's feedback (Taxes & Financial Fundamentals). Additional modules Brian proposed (Go-to-Market, Hiring & Immigration, Location & Market Strategy) are positioned as future sprint modules.

**Module 1 — Getting Started in the U.S.**
- Choosing the right business entity (LLC, C-Corp, S-Corp)
- Registering in Florida (Sunbiz), obtaining an EIN
- Opening a U.S. business bank account
- Initial compliance and insurance requirements

**Module 2 — U.S. Business Culture**
- Communication norms and expectations
- Meeting etiquette and relationship-building
- Negotiation styles and decision-making speed
- Common cultural missteps international founders make

**Module 3 — Protecting Your Business (IP)**
- Overview of U.S. intellectual property: copyright, trademark, patent
- Common mistakes international founders make with IP
- When and how to bring in an attorney
- Contract basics and risk awareness

**Module 4 — Finding Support & Resources**
- Key organizations: SBDC, SBA, chambers of commerce, economic development agencies
- What each offers and when to engage them
- Navigating the Central Florida ecosystem
- UCF BIP as part of the resource landscape (not a dedicated sales module — integrated naturally)

**Module 5 — Taxes & Financial Fundamentals**
- Federal and Florida tax obligations for foreign-owned businesses
- Sales tax, payroll basics, estimated payments
- Financial reporting expectations
- When to engage an accountant vs. attorney

*Content for Modules 1-4 will draw from Brian's provided source materials (OpenMyFloridaBusiness.gov, SBDC, SelectFlorida, CFDC, Orlando Economic Partnership, and other links shared). Module 5 will require additional source review. Principles go in-course; regulatory specifics link out to authoritative sources.*

## 4. Platform & Hosting — Pilot Approach

### Why Not LearnDash

The original proposal recommended LearnDash ($29-69/month paid by UCF) as the hosting platform. Based on what we now know about the requirements — business profiling, routing logic, stakeholder analytics, data export — a standard LMS like LearnDash cannot support these features without significant custom plugin development.

### What We Are Offering Instead

For this pilot, we will host the program on our proprietary development platform. This platform was built specifically for this type of learning experience and includes capabilities that would otherwise require custom development or enterprise-grade LMS tools:

| Capability | What It Does |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Business Profile Intake** | Configurable questionnaire at course start — captures business stage, industry, origin, goals, language preference |
| **Program Matching** | Rule-based routing that maps learner profiles to recommended next steps (exit / learn more / connect with Brian) |
| **Consultation Booking** | Tiered call-to-action at course completion — integrates with scheduling tools (Calendly, Cal.com, or custom link) |
| **Adaptive Content** | Ability to emphasize or surface content based on the learner's profile responses |
| **Impact Dashboard** | Stakeholder-facing analytics: aggregate profiles, completion rates, satisfaction scores, referral data |
| **Data Export** | Learner data export via CSV or webhook to external systems (CRM, spreadsheet, Zapier) |
| **Localization** | Multi-language architecture — Spanish support can be enabled by uploading translated content (no rebuild required) |
| **Payment Processing** | Built-in Stripe and PayPal integration for collecting the ~$100 course fee |
| **Certificates** | Completion certificates with optional blockchain verification |

### Pilot Terms

This platform hosting is included as part of the $7,500 engagement for the pilot period. Here is how we position it:

- **For the pilot:** We host, manage, and maintain the platform at no additional cost to UCF BIP. This gives us both a live environment to validate the approach, test with real users, and collect the data Brian needs to demonstrate impact.
- **After the pilot:** We evaluate together what the best long-term path looks like. Options include continued hosting on our platform (at a service fee to be determined based on usage), migration to a UCF-managed LMS, or export as a standalone SCORM package. The course content is SCORM-compatible and is not locked into our platform.
- **Data ownership:** All learner data collected during the pilot belongs to UCF BIP. We provide full export at any time.

### What We Need From You: Expected User Volume

To configure the platform appropriately and set realistic expectations for stakeholder reporting, we need to understand:

- **How many learners do you expect in the first 3 months?** (e.g., 50? 100? 500?)
- **How many in the first year?**
- **Will there be cohort-based enrollment (e.g., tied to events like the Hungarian Hub on April 17) or continuous open enrollment?**
- **Is there a target number Brian wants to report to stakeholders?** (e.g., "100 international entrepreneurs served in Q2")

This does not change the cost — it helps us configure capacity, set up appropriate analytics baselines, and design the intake flow for the expected volume.
