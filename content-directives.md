Every module (1 through 7) MUST include a **"Watch Your Money: What This Actually Costs"** callout with realistic cost ranges relevant to that module's topic. Module 0 (Welcome) does NOT get a Watch Your Money callout — no specific dollar amounts in the Welcome module.

### Cost Policy

### Required Disclaimers (exact text from Brian)

**1. Legal/Educational Disclaimer**
> This program provides general educational information to help international founders understand the U.S. business environment. It does not constitute legal, tax, immigration, or financial advice. Participants should consult qualified professionals before making business decisions.

**Placement:** MUST appear in lesson-0-1 (prominently, near the top). MUST be re-emphasized in lesson-8-3 (Next Steps).

**2. Immigration Disclaimer**

It is necessary to consult with qualified immigration experts for more details and before making any decisions about your visa status or immigration pathway.

**3. Visa Disclaimer**

**Placement:** MUST appear in lesson-4-2 (Visa Pathways) as a prominent callout at the top of the lesson.

**Placement:** MUST appear in lesson-0-1, near the top, before content begins.

## Part D: Content Quality Standards (from Module 1 Review, Mar 18)

> **Source:** QA review of Module 1 generated content. These rules prevent recurring quality issues.
> **Authority:** `experience-design.json` (voiceCandor, themeIntegrationRules, funFactsEasterEggs.qualityRules, lessonTransitions) and `strategy.json` (contentGenerationGuardRails)

### Theme Integration
- [ ] Landscape theme is INSTRUCTIONAL, not decorative — the analogy must help explain a concept, not just label a setting
- [ ] Each lesson after the first in a multi-lesson module opens with a one-sentence transition connecting it to the previous lesson (see `experience-design.json → lessonTransitions`)
- [ ] Tier 1 modules (1, 2, 4, 7): theme referenced at least 2-3 times across the lesson, tied to content
- [ ] Tier 2 modules (3, 5, 6): theme in opening + fun fact only — no forced analogies
- [ ] TEST: Remove all theme references. If the lesson reads identically, the theme is decorative and needs rework.

### Fun Facts
- [ ] Each fun fact is SPECIFIC and SURPRISING — not a generic stat that could describe any U.S. metro
- [ ] Fun facts within the same module are DISTINCT — never restate the same data point
- [ ] Fun facts are TIED TO THE LESSON TOPIC — banking lesson gets a banking fact, not a formation fact
- [ ] Prefer fun facts that are also USEFUL (insider tips) over pure trivia

### Voice Candor
- [ ] At least 2 founder anecdotes per module — specific stories with country of origin and outcome
- [ ] Content does NOT immediately reassure after presenting a difficult topic — let the difficulty breathe for 1-2 sentences
- [ ] At least one moment of insider personality per module ("here is what they do not tell you," "this part is genuinely frustrating")
- [ ] Avoid the flat pattern: state issue → bullet list → qualifier → move on

### Callout Balance
- [ ] No lesson has more than 3 callout blocks
- [ ] No two callouts in the same lesson deliver the same message (merge overlapping)
- [ ] Teaching-to-callout ratio is healthy: more text blocks than callout blocks in every lesson

### Brand & Endorsement
- [ ] No brand name appears more than twice in a single lesson (text + callout combined)
- [ ] Brand mentions are informational, not promotional — "platforms like Mercury" not "we recommend Mercury"

### Content Depth
- [ ] The hardest topic for founders in each module gets the most teaching content (text blocks + word count)
- [ ] No lesson on a difficult topic (banking, taxes, cultural missteps) should be shorter than lessons on easier topics in the same module
